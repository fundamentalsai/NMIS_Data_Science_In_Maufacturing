General Questions

A

This is a comprehensive 10-week course designed to teach data science and Python programming specifically for manufacturing professionals. You'll learn to analyze manufacturing data, build machine learning models, work with databases, process 3D point clouds, implement process mining, and integrate with manufacturing systems like PLM, ERP, and MES.

A

This course is designed for manufacturing professionals including engineers, managers, quality specialists, production planners, and anyone working in the manufacturing industry who wants to leverage data science. No prior programming or data science experience is required.

A

The course runs for 10 weeks with one 3-hour session every Friday from 10:00am to 1:00pm. Each session includes a 90-minute lecture followed by a 90-minute hands-on workshop with short breaks throughout.

A

The course is conducted both online via Zoom and through on-campus sessions at the National Manufacturing Institute Scotland (NMIS) in Glasgow, UK. This hybrid format allows flexibility for remote participants while providing hands-on experiences for those who can attend in person.

A

All sessions are recorded and made available to enrolled students within 24 hours. You can watch the recordings at your convenience. However, we strongly encourage live attendance to benefit from real-time Q&A and interactive workshops.

A

A

The next cohort begins in early May. Check the Topics & Schedule page for exact dates. We typically run 2-3 cohorts per year. Apply early as spaces are limited to ensure quality instruction and personalized support.

A

Yes, we maintain manageable class sizes to ensure everyone receives adequate attention during workshops and seminars. This fosters a collaborative learning environment where participants can network and learn from each other, as professionals from leading manufacturing companies have done in previous cohorts.

Course Content & Materials

A

The course covers: Introduction to data-driven manufacturing, data quality and data carpentry, Python programming fundamentals, NumPy and Pandas libraries, product lifecycle and material flows (PLM), data visualization and exploratory data analysis (EDA), data cleaning, SQL and manufacturing databases (PLM, ERP, MES), Git version control, machine learning algorithms (regression, classification, clustering), process mining and process analysis, and 3D data and point cloud analysis.

A

Yes! All enrolled students receive comprehensive materials including presentation slides, Jupyter notebooks for each workshop, manufacturing datasets for practice, code examples, and documentation resources. All materials can be downloaded as bundles (presentations, notebooks, datasets, and recorded videos) and remain accessible after course completion.

A

Absolutely! Each week includes hands-on workshops and seminars where you'll apply what you've learned to real manufacturing datasets. Expert instructors including Dr. Andrew Sherlock, Dr. Olga Bylya, Safi Sohail Azim, and Sharva Kumar Staya Kasarapu guide you through exercises and are available to help with any challenges.

A

Yes! All exercises use real (anonymized) manufacturing datasets from various industries including automotive, aerospace, electronics, and precision engineering. You'll work with sensor data, production logs, quality inspection records, material flow data, and more.

A

Yes! All course materials, including downloadable presentations, Jupyter notebooks, datasets, and video recordings remain accessible to you after course completion. You can revisit lessons, practice with datasets, and use the materials as reference for your work projects.

Technical Requirements

A

You'll need Python (we recommend Anaconda distribution), a code editor (VS Code or Jupyter Notebook), Git, and access to databases for SQL exercises. All software is free and open-source. We provide detailed installation guides and setup support in Week 1 to ensure everyone is ready to go.

A

A laptop or desktop with Windows, macOS, or Linux. Minimum 8GB RAM (16GB recommended), 20GB free disk space, and a stable internet connection (at least 5 Mbps). Most modern computers from the last 5 years will work fine.

A

Yes, but check with your IT department first. Some corporate networks have restrictions on installing software or accessing certain websites. We recommend getting approval to install Python, Git, and access GitHub and Zoom before the course starts.

A

Yes! Instructors hold weekly office hours for one-on-one help. We also provide dedicated tutor support for questions and doubts throughout the course. Our experienced team is always available to assist with technical challenges.

A

Don't worry! With recorded sessions, you can catch up at your own pace. Instructors are available during office hours to help you understand concepts you missed. The course materials are self-contained, so you can work through them independently if needed.

Certificates & Completion

A

Yes! Upon successful completion of the course, you'll receive a certificate from NMIS (National Manufacturing Institute Scotland) recognizing your achievement. The certificate includes the topics covered and can be added to your CV and LinkedIn profile.

A

To receive a certificate, you must attend (live or via recording) at least 8 out of 10 sessions and actively participate in hands-on workshops. Each week includes practical exercises where you'll apply data science techniques to real manufacturing datasets from companies like Bacardi, Rolls-Royce, Spirit AeroSystems, and others.

A

The certificate is issued by NMIS, a recognized center of excellence in manufacturing. While not a formal academic qualification, it's highly valued by manufacturing employers and demonstrates practical data science skills applicable to industrial settings.

A

You'll join our alumni network, gaining access to exclusive events, advanced workshops, and job opportunities. We also offer ongoing support through our community forum and occasional refresher sessions on new tools and techniques in manufacturing data science.

A

Alumni can audit future cohorts at a significantly reduced rate (or sometimes free if space permits). This is useful if you want a refresher, missed certain topics, or want to deepen your understanding with additional practice.
