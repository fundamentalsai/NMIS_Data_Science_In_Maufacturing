General Questions

A

This is a comprehensive 10-week course designed to teach data science and Python programming specifically for manufacturing professionals. You'll learn to analyze manufacturing data, build predictive models, work with IoT sensors, process 3D point clouds, and integrate with manufacturing systems like ERP and PLM.

A

This course is designed for manufacturing professionals including engineers, managers, quality specialists, production planners, and anyone working in the manufacturing industry who wants to leverage data science. No prior programming or data science experience is required.

A

The course runs for 10 weeks with one 3-hour session every Friday from 10:00am to 1:00pm. Each session includes a 90-minute lecture followed by a 90-minute hands-on workshop with short breaks throughout.

A

The course is conducted entirely online via Zoom, making it accessible to participants from anywhere. This format allows you to learn from the comfort of your home or office while still benefiting from live interaction with instructors and peers.

A

All sessions are recorded and made available to enrolled students within 24 hours. You can watch the recordings at your convenience. However, we strongly encourage live attendance to benefit from real-time Q&A and interactive workshops.

Enrollment & Registration

A

Click the "Apply" button in the navigation menu or on any page to access the application form. Fill out your details including your name, email, company, and role. You'll receive a confirmation email with next steps within 2 business days.

A

There are no prerequisites! This course is designed for complete beginners. No coding experience, no data science background, and no advanced mathematics are required. We start from the basics and build up progressively.

A

Course fees vary depending on enrollment type (individual vs. corporate sponsorship) and early bird discounts. Please contact us at fundamentalsai.nmis@gmail.com for current pricing and any available discounts for multiple participants from the same organization.

A

The next cohort begins in early May. Check the Topics & Schedule page for exact dates. We typically run 2-3 cohorts per year. Apply early as spaces are limited to ensure quality instruction and personalized support.

A

Yes, we limit each cohort to 25-30 participants to ensure everyone receives adequate attention during workshops and Q&A sessions. This also fosters a collaborative learning environment where participants can network and learn from each other.

Course Content & Materials

A

The course covers: Python programming fundamentals, data structures and objects, NumPy and Pandas, data visualization, exploratory data analysis, data cleaning, manufacturing databases and ERP/PLM systems, Git version control, machine learning (regression, classification, PCA), digital twins, IoT, process mining, and 3D point cloud analysis.

A

Yes! All enrolled students receive comprehensive materials including presentation slides (PDF and PPTX), Jupyter notebooks for each workshop, datasets for practice, code examples, and additional reading resources. Materials are shared 24 hours before each session and remain accessible after course completion.

A

Absolutely! Each week includes a 90-minute hands-on workshop where you'll apply what you've learned to real manufacturing datasets. Instructors guide you through exercises and are available to help with any challenges. You'll build a portfolio of practical projects by the end of the course.

A

Yes! All exercises use real (anonymized) manufacturing datasets from various industries including automotive, aerospace, electronics, and precision engineering. You'll work with sensor data, production logs, quality inspection records, material flow data, and more.

A

Yes! All course materials, recordings, and resources remain accessible to you for 12 months after course completion. You can revisit lessons, practice with datasets, and use the materials as reference for your work projects.

Technical Requirements

A

You'll need Python (we recommend Anaconda distribution), a code editor (VS Code or Jupyter), and Git. All software is free and open-source. We provide detailed installation guides and have a setup session in Week 1 to ensure everyone is ready to go.

A

A laptop or desktop with Windows, macOS, or Linux. Minimum 8GB RAM (16GB recommended), 20GB free disk space, and a stable internet connection (at least 5 Mbps). Most modern computers from the last 5 years will work fine.

A

Yes, but check with your IT department first. Some corporate networks have restrictions on installing software or accessing certain websites. We recommend getting approval to install Python, Git, and access GitHub and Zoom before the course starts.

A

Yes! We have a dedicated discussion forum where you can ask questions between sessions. Instructors also hold weekly office hours for one-on-one help. During live sessions, teaching assistants are available to troubleshoot technical issues.

A

Don't worry! With recorded sessions, you can catch up at your own pace. Instructors are available during office hours to help you understand concepts you missed. The course materials are self-contained, so you can work through them independently if needed.

Certificates & Completion

A

Yes! Upon successful completion of the course, you'll receive a certificate from NMIS (National Manufacturing Institute Scotland) recognizing your achievement. The certificate includes the topics covered and can be added to your CV and LinkedIn profile.

A

To receive a certificate, you must attend (live or via recording) at least 8 out of 10 sessions and complete a final project. The final project involves applying what you've learned to a manufacturing dataset of your choice, with guidance from instructors.

A

The certificate is issued by NMIS, a recognized center of excellence in manufacturing. While not a formal academic qualification, it's highly valued by manufacturing employers and demonstrates practical data science skills applicable to industrial settings.

A

You'll join our alumni network, gaining access to exclusive events, advanced workshops, and job opportunities. We also offer ongoing support through our community forum and occasional refresher sessions on new tools and techniques in manufacturing data science.

A

Alumni can audit future cohorts at a significantly reduced rate (or sometimes free if space permits). This is useful if you want a refresher, missed certain topics, or want to deepen your understanding with additional practice.
